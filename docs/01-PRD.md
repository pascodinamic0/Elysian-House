# Product Requirements Document
## Elysian House — Transform & Thrive: A Gathering for Women Ready to Bloom

---

## 1. Problem Statement

There is a specific moment in a woman's life when everything looks fine — functioning career, stable relationships, external success — but something inside has gone quiet. She's not broken. She's not in crisis. She's **outgrowing**.

Most spaces designed for women in transition fail her:
- Therapy intellectualizes without witnessing
- Self-help speaks *at* her, not *with* her
- Wellness spaces feel performative or spiritually prescriptive
- Corporate women's events feel transactional
- Online communities lack embodied presence

**She needs a room. A real one. With other women who understand without explanation.**

Elysian House exists to hold that room.

This website is not a marketing funnel. It is the **first threshold** — the digital equivalent of walking up to a door and feeling, before it opens, that something true is on the other side.

---

## 2. Target User Archetypes

### Primary: "The Quiet Outgrower"

**Demographics:**
- 28–45 years old
- Urban, educated, emotionally literate
- Likely in Dubai or willing to travel for meaningful experiences

**Psychographics:**
- High-functioning, often described as "the strong one"
- Has done inner work (therapy, journaling, self-reflection) but still feels unseen
- Consumes thoughtful content: On Being, The Mountain Is You, Women Who Run With the Wolves
- Instagram over TikTok; saves posts privately, rarely comments
- Skeptical of anything that feels like a sales pitch or spiritual performance

**Emotional State:**
- Not lost — transitioning
- Not broken — outgrowing
- Not seeking answers — seeking witness
- Tired of carrying everything alone and calling it strength

**Core Fear:**
That this will be another space that disappoints — too woo, too surface, too performative, or secretly a funnel.

**Core Desire:**
To be in a room where she doesn't have to explain herself. Where "I don't know what I want yet" is a valid starting point.

### Secondary: "The Curious Observer"

- Follows Elysian House content but isn't ready to attend
- May join the newsletter or waitlist
- Needs multiple touchpoints before commitment
- The website must feel valuable even if she never registers

## 3. User Journey

### Before the Website
- Encounters Elysian House through Instagram content
- Language resonates: "If your life looks fine but feels wrong..."
- Clicks link in bio or saves for later

### Landing on the Website
- **First 3 seconds:** Visual calm. No aggression. No urgency. She exhales slightly.
- **First 10 seconds:** Reads a line that names her inner experience. Feels seen, not sold to.
- **First 30 seconds:** Understands this is a gathering, not a seminar. Facilitated, not lecture-based.
- **First 2 minutes:** Scrolls through the narrative. Each section deepens resonance without repeating.

### Decision Point
- She either:
  - **Requests an invitation** (high intent, ready)
  - **Joins the waitlist / follows** (interested, not ready)
  - **Leaves but remembers** (the site stays with her)

### Post-Registration
- Receives a confirmation that feels like a letter, not a receipt
- One follow-up email that reinforces the tone
- No aggressive reminders. No countdown urgency.

### At the Event
- The physical space matches the digital promise
- She thinks: "This is exactly what I expected it to feel like"

### After the Event
- She returns to the website to share it
- The site still feels true — not a bait-and-switch

## 4. Key Outcomes the Website Must Create

### Emotional Outcomes
1. **Recognition** — "They're describing something I've never been able to articulate"
2. **Permission** — "I'm allowed to want more without being ungrateful"
3. **Safety** — "This doesn't feel like it's going to manipulate me"
4. **Belonging** — "There are other women like me"
5. **Curiosity** — "I want to know what happens in that room"

### Functional Outcomes
1. Clearly communicate what the event is (and isn't)
2. Establish Elysian House as a brand with longevity, not a one-off
3. Capture registrations with minimal friction
4. Build an email list of aligned future attendees
5. Provide enough information without over-explaining

---

## 5. Non-Goals (What We Are NOT Building)

- ❌ A personal brand website for the founder
- ❌ A coaching or services sales page
- ❌ A content-heavy blog or editorial platform (yet)
- ❌ A community portal or member login
- ❌ A multi-event booking system
- ❌ An e-commerce experience
- ❌ Anything that requires the user to "learn" the interface

---

## 6. Success Metrics

### Qualitative
- Users describe the site as "calming," "different," "felt like me"
- No feedback suggesting it feels "salesy" or "like everything else"
- Attendees say the event matched the website's promise
- The founder feels proud to share the link

### Quantitative
- Registration conversion rate from unique visitors (target: 8-12% for qualified traffic)
- Average time on site > 2 minutes (indicates reading, not bouncing)
- Scroll depth > 70% on main page
- Email list growth from secondary CTA
- Low bounce rate from Instagram referrals (< 40%)

---

## 7. Constraints & Boundaries

### Design Constraints
- No generic hero patterns
- No gradient backgrounds
- No stock photography
- No aggressive CTAs ("Limited spots!" "Register NOW!")
- No testimonial carousels
- No floating chat widgets
- No pop-ups

### Content Constraints
- Copy must be first-person plural ("we") or second-person intimate ("you")
- Never diagnostic ("If you're struggling with...")
- Never promissory ("You WILL transform")
- Always invitational ("You're welcome here")

### Technical Constraints
- Must work flawlessly on mobile (primary traffic source: Instagram)
- Page load < 2 seconds
- Accessible (WCAG AA minimum)
- No required JavaScript for core content
